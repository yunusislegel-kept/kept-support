1. Who we are

"Kept" refers to the macOS clipboard manager app and the team that develops it ("we", "us", "our"). This Privacy Policy applies to the Kept app for macOS and the Kept support pages hosted on this site. You can reach us at keptsupport@gmail.com.

2. What the app collects

The Kept app itself does not collect, transmit, sell, rent, or share personal data with the developer. There is no account, no sign-in, no profile, no telemetry, no usage analytics, and no advertising identifier.

The only time we ever see personal data is when you choose to email us at keptsupport@gmail.com. That exchange is covered in Section 11 — Support emails.

3. Local clipboard history

Kept is a local-first macOS clipboard manager. Clipboard items, boards, searches, filters, capture rules, settings, pinned items, local snapshots, and backups are designed to remain on your Mac. Managed PDFs, images, GIFs, supported videos, editor working files, thumbnails, and exports also remain in Kept's local sandbox unless you explicitly export, paste, or share them.

▣

Local Library container

Kept stores its app data locally inside the macOS Application Support / Library container. The developer has no access to this local container.

⌁

No cloud sync

Kept does not automatically synchronize clipboard history between devices. Moving data to another Mac requires a user-controlled manual export and import.

4. Network, analytics, ads, tracking

The Kept app does not use third-party analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, tracking technologies, behavioral profiling, or cross-app/cross-site identifiers. Clipboard content is not uploaded to remote servers for processing.

⌘

No network access for clipboard data

Clipboard capture, search, rules, local snapshots, and backups are designed to work on your Mac.

✕

No tracking

Kept does not track users across apps or websites and does not share data with advertising networks.

5. Supported content types and PDF processing

Depending on how you use the app, Kept may locally save supported clipboard content such as:

  • Text and links
  • Code snippets
  • Images and small previews (PNG, JPEG, HEIC)
  • PDF clipboard items
  • Animated GIFs (animation preserved)
  • Colors copied from system color picker or design tools
  • Contact cards (vCard) copied by the user
  • Calendar events (ICS) copied by the user
  • Files or file references supported by macOS clipboard behavior

Kept uses local safety caps to avoid exhausting memory or disk space: PDFs up to 250 MB, images up to 40 MB, animated GIFs up to 12 MB, and supported videos up to 500 MB. A file above its cap must be reduced or split before it can be managed by Kept.

⌘

PDF processing stays on the Mac

Kept copies a captured PDF into its sandbox and processes pages, text, images, object edits, thumbnails, saves, and exports on device. PDF content is not uploaded to a Kept or third-party processing server.

80

Bounded large-document sessions

For PDFs above 80 pages, PDF Studio loads the first 80 pages in one editor session and displays both loaded and total page counts. The managed original is preserved.

!

Files that cannot be opened safely

Empty or damaged PDFs, missing managed copies, documents macOS cannot decode, and some password- or permission-protected files are refused instead of being rewritten unsafely.

✓

Unsupported edits fail closed

Complex structures, restricted permissions, or fonts and glyphs that cannot be replaced safely may limit editing. Kept blocks the unsafe change and retains the original PDF layer.

6. Sensitive content protection

Kept includes privacy features intended to reduce accidental exposure of sensitive clipboard items. These features run locally and can be adjusted by you.

✋

Hidden copies

Copies marked by the source app as concealed or autogenerated (e.g. by password managers) can be skipped by default from normal views.

◇

Smart Secret Guard

Kept can detect common secret-like patterns such as API keys, bearer tokens, JWTs, private keys, passwords, and payment card numbers, then store them in a more protected way.

⏱

Short-lived sensitive items

Items marked as temporary or sensitive can be automatically removed after a short time, depending on your privacy settings.

◌

Private mode

Kept can temporarily pause capture before sensitive work, so clipboard changes are not read while private mode is active.

7. Protected sources and rules

Kept lets you exclude or protect specific source applications and websites. For example, you can avoid saving content copied from password managers, private messaging apps, terminal sessions, protected websites, or other sensitive sources.

8. Backups, exports, imports

Kept does not automatically export or synchronize your clipboard history. Exports and backups are manual actions controlled by you. If you choose to export Kept data, the exported file is your responsibility and should be stored securely.

9. Data deletion

You can clear clipboard history, remove saved items, delete local backups, and reset saved data from inside the app where available. You may also remove Kept's local macOS application container (~/Library/Containers/com.yunusislegel.kept) to delete the local library. Kept also stores a random Support ID and free editor counters in Keychain; these records contain no clipboard or PDF content. To remove them, use Keychain Access to delete com.kept.support and com.yunusislegel.kept.editUsage.v1.

10. Permissions

Kept runs inside the macOS App Sandbox with the hardened runtime. The app does not request access to location, camera, microphone, contacts, calendar, or direct network services. Anything saved in Kept comes from your clipboard actions and from macOS clipboard behavior. Opening this support site or composing an email is an explicit user action handled by your browser or mail app. Apple separately handles App Store purchases.

11. Support emails

When you write to keptsupport@gmail.com — for support, bug reports, feedback, or questions — that email arrives in a Gmail inbox operated by the Kept team. As with any email exchange, we receive:

  • The email address you send from.
  • The subject line and message body, including any attachments you choose to add.
  • Standard email metadata (timestamps, message IDs) added by your email provider.

We use this information solely to read your message, reply to you, and improve Kept based on what we learn. We do not sell support emails, do not feed them into advertising systems, and do not share them with third parties except where legally required.

⌗

Where it lives

Emails are stored in Gmail and processed by Google as our email host. Google's privacy and security practices apply to that storage in addition to ours.

⏱

How long we keep it

We keep support emails as long as it is reasonably useful to provide ongoing support, fix related bugs, or maintain a record of the conversation. You can ask us to delete your email thread at any time.

⚖

Legal basis (EU / UK)

We rely on our legitimate interest in answering support requests and improving the product, and on your consent — implied by you choosing to email us. You can withdraw consent by asking us to stop processing or delete the thread.

🛈

Please avoid sensitive data

Please do not include passwords, API keys, payment card numbers, or other secrets in support emails. If you do, we will only use them to reproduce the issue you reported and will delete them when the issue is resolved.

12. Third-party services

The Kept app does not send clipboard history or personal data to third-party analytics, advertising, tracking, or cloud-processing services. The Kept support site is hosted on GitHub Pages and uses Gmail (Google) for inbound support email — these providers act only as infrastructure and do not receive clipboard data from the app. Support emails include a locally generated Support ID plus app version, macOS version, and language when you choose to compose the message; no clipboard or PDF file is attached automatically. The App Store handles purchases according to Apple's own privacy policy.

13. Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Because the Kept app does not collect personal data, most data-subject rights apply only to information you sent us via email. If you are a resident of the EU, UK, EEA, California, or another jurisdiction with comparable rights, you can:

  • Ask what personal data we hold about you (access).
  • Ask us to correct or update it (rectification).
  • Ask us to delete your support thread and any attachments (erasure).
  • Ask us to stop processing your data (restriction / objection).
  • Ask for a copy of your support thread (portability).

To exercise any of these rights, write to keptsupport@gmail.com. We aim to reply within 30 days. You also have the right to complain to your local data protection authority.

14. Children's privacy

Kept is not directed at children under 13 and is not intended for use by them. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has sent us personal data via email, contact us and we will delete it.

15. Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if Kept's features or data handling practices change. The latest version will always be available at this URL, and the "Last updated" date at the top of the page will reflect the most recent revision.

16. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, need support, or want to exercise your data rights, please reach us at: keptsupport@gmail.com.
